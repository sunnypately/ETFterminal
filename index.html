Coverage Methodology

// how this universe was built

The coverage is intentionally small and intentionally boring. The thesis of this terminal is that for the long-horizon investor, ETF selection is largely solved — the durable alpha is in position sizing, fee discipline, tax-aware location, and behavioral consistency. The 18 names here meet every screen we apply.

Inclusion criteria

Every name in coverage passes the same screens: (i) sponsor of meaningful scale (Vanguard, BlackRock/iShares, Schwab, State Street/SPDR, Invesco, Avantis), (ii) AUM sufficient to make closure risk negligible, (iii) expense ratio at or near category leader, (iv) a transparent, rules-based index methodology, (v) deep secondary-market liquidity, and (vi) a thesis that survives a full market cycle.

Names that score well on five of six but fail one — for example, a popular thematic ETF with a 0.75% expense ratio — are deliberately excluded. The cost compounds against you for thirty years; the thesis may not survive five.

What we excluded — and why

Thematic ETFs (AI, robotics, cannabis, space, blockchain) are absent by design. Their fees are punitive, their methodologies often re-rank into whatever just rallied, and their staying power is conditional on the theme remaining narratively in vogue. They may belong in a satellite trade; they do not belong in a long-horizon core.

Leveraged and inverse ETFs (TQQQ, SOXL, FNGU, SQQQ) are explicitly excluded from the long-horizon coverage. Daily reset volatility decay makes them structurally inappropriate for buy-and-hold — they are trading instruments, and we cover them in a separate signals book, not here.

Actively managed ETFs without a quantitative process are excluded because the fee/performance tradeoff is unfavorable over multi-decade windows. Where active management earns its fee — small-cap value (AVUV), for example — it is included.

How to use this terminal

Start with Tab 03 (Model Portfolios) to anchor an allocation framework. Use Tab 02 (Simulator) to stress-test compounding assumptions against your contribution schedule and time horizon. Return to Tab 01 (Core Coverage) to pressure-test individual line items against the thesis.

The hardest part of long-horizon investing is not selection — it is doing nothing for thirty years while the position works. Build the portfolio so that "do nothing" is a defensible posture, then defend it.
